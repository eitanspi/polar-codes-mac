## Figure index

### Fig 4 — `fig_isi_mac_bler.{png,pdf}` (HEADLINE)

**Caption.** ISI-MAC (h = 0.3, SNR = 6 dB): block-error rate versus
block length N for the proposed chained neural polar decoder (green)
compared with the analytical chained trellis SC decoder (blue), the
joint trellis SC decoder (cyan), the memoryless SC decoder (purple),
and the previous scalar NPD baseline (grey, Stage 1 only). The neural
chained NPD matches the analytical chained trellis SC at N = 16 and 32
and is within 1.5x at N = 64, all without explicit knowledge of the
channel state. Error bars are Wilson 95% CIs on 2000 codewords per
point.

**Data sources.**
- Trellis/joint-trellis/memoryless SC baselines:
  `class_c_npd/results/chained_trellis_sc_isi_mac.json`
- Chained NPD (neural, ours): best-per-N from
  `class_c_npd/results/npd_memory_mac_results.md`
  (window W=2 at N=16,32; BiGRU L=1 at N=64)
- Scalar NPD (prior baseline):
  `class_c_npd/results/isi_mac_classC_npd.json`

### Fig 1 — `fig_ncg_bler_gmac_B.{png,pdf}`

**Caption.** GMAC Class B (symmetric sum rate 0.96, SNR = 6 dB): block
error rate versus block length N for SC (blue) and the Neural
Closed-Graph (NCG) decoder (red). NCG tracks SC at N <= 128 but the
2000-codeword evaluation at N = 256 reveals a plateau — the "NCG wall"
— at 4.5x SC, and NCG was not trained for N = 512, 1024 so SC-only
data is shown. Error bars are Wilson 95% CIs.

**Data sources.**
- NCG vs SC at N=32,64,128,256:
  `project_summary/results/gmac_classB_ncg_vs_sc.csv`
- SC-only at N=512, 1024: class-B campaign logs summarised in
  `project_summary/NCG_CHAPTER.md`

### Fig 2 — `fig_ncg_bler_bemac_B.{png,pdf}`

**Caption.** BEMAC Class B (sum rate = 1, binary erasure MAC): block
error rate versus block length N for SC (blue) and NCG (red). NCG
matches or beats SC at all N from 16 to 1024. At N = 256 (50 000
codewords) NCG reaches half the SC BLER (4e-5 vs 8e-5). Error bars
are Wilson 95% CIs.

**Data source.** `project_summary/results/bemac_classB_ncg_vs_sc.csv`.

### Fig 3 — `fig_chained_npd_corner_gmac.{png,pdf}`

**Caption.** GMAC Class C at the corner rate (approx 50% of capacity):
block error rate versus block length N for SC (blue) and the Chained
Neural Polar Decoder (green). At N = 512 the chained NPD cuts SC BLER
from 5e-4 to 2.4e-4 (0.48x) using 50 000 codewords. Error bars are
Wilson 95% CIs.

**Data source.** `project_summary/results/gmac_classC_npd_vs_sc.csv`.

### Fig 5 — `fig_rate_sweep_n256.{png,pdf}`

**Caption.** GMAC Class B rate sweep at N = 64, 128, 256 (SNR = 6 dB,
5000/3000 codewords per point): BLER vs total sum rate R_U + R_V for
SC (dashed, open markers) and NCG (solid). NCG closes on SC up to
about 0.78 bits/use at every N; at higher rates and at N = 256 the
NCG curve lifts well above SC, confirming the N = 256 wall as a
multi-rate phenomenon (not a single-rate artefact). Wilson 95% CIs
are included in the source JSON.

**Data source.** `results/gmac_classB_rate_sweep.json`.

### Fig 6 — `fig_first_error_n256.{png,pdf}`

**Caption.** First-error analysis of NCG at N = 256 (5000 codewords,
115 block failures). Panel (a) shows a histogram of the SC step index
at which the first bit error occurs; most errors cluster in the last
quarter of the decoding schedule (steps 180-248). Panel (b) splits
first-error events by user (U vs V) and shows their per-user
information-bit index: errors are concentrated in a handful of
high-rate positions near the top of each user's reliable set.

**Data source.** `/tmp/first_err_n256.npz` (produced by
`scripts/first_error_analysis.py`).

### Fig 7 — `fig_mi_per_position_n32.{png,pdf}`

**Caption.** Per-position mutual information at N = 32 for GMAC Class
B rate-1 synthesis training (3 seeds, 200 000 samples per seed). The
two curves are NCG's estimate of I(U_i; Z, past) and I(V_i; Z, past,
U) at each information-bit index (in bit-reversal order). Both curves
show the characteristic polarisation profile: many near-0 positions
(to be frozen), a transition region, and many near-1 positions (the
reliable set). The U and V curves are asymmetric because the
successive-cancellation schedule decodes U before V.

**Data source.**
`class_c_npd/results/ncg_r1_32/mi_per_pos_reliable.json`
(mi_u_mean, mi_v_mean fields; file is slightly truncated but the two
mean arrays are intact).

### Bonus: `fig_lower_rate_experiment.{png,pdf}`

**Caption.** GMAC Class B N = 256 "freeze-top-K-weakest" experiment:
starting from the symmetric rate (k_u = k_v = 123, the NCG wall
operating point) we progressively freeze the K positions with
highest NCG BLER contribution. Panel (a) shows SC and NCG BLER across
K = 0, 5, 10, 20. Panel (b) shows the NCG/SC ratio: dropping the top
5 positions halves the ratio (5.2x -> 3.3x), but continuing to K = 20
the gap widens again to 10.5x because SC captures the full
information content of every removed position while NCG only captures
some. The deficit is therefore NOT concentrated in a small fixable
set.

**Data source.** `/tmp/exp_freeze_weakest_results.npz` (ratios and
k-values); absolute BLERs from the prose in
`project_summary/NCG_CHAPTER.md` at the corresponding operating
points.

### Bonus: `fig_training_curve_n32.{png,pdf}`

**Caption.** Training trajectory of the NCG rate-1 POC at N = 32:
cross-entropy loss stays at its random-initialisation value (~ 1.04)
for approximately 230 000 iterations, then undergoes a phase
transition over the next 30 000 iterations during which the loss
drops from 1.04 to 0.45. At 300 000 iterations training was still
improving. This delayed-onset phenomenon motivates the curriculum
(N=16 -> 32 -> 64 -> ...) used throughout the rest of the work:
training from scratch is prohibitively expensive at larger N.

**Data source.** `class_c_npd/results/ncg_r1_32.log` (every 10 000
iter evaluation).

## Style summary

All figures use:
- serif font, size 11 (labels 12, legend 9)
- log-scale y-axis for BLER plots
- solid lines with markers (o, s, D, ^) for decoders
- colour scheme: SC blue (#1f77b4), NCG red (#d62728), Chained NPD
  green (#2ca02c), CRC-SCL orange (#ff7f0e), memoryless SC purple
  (#9467bd), joint trellis SC cyan (#17becf), scalar NPD grey
  (#7f7f7f)
- Wilson 95% CIs as error bars when n_codewords is known
- minor gridlines (dotted, alpha 0.2) on both axes
- legend position chosen per plot to avoid data overlap
- tight bbox; 300 DPI for PNG; vector PDF
